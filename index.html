featured luau: chief's luau

Visiting a Hawaiian luau is one of the top things to do in Hawaii. This Hawaiian tradition is the perfect way to immerse yourself in island culture, try Hawaiian cuisine and enjoy music and dances from Hawaii and other Polynesian islands, such as Tahiti, Samoa and Tonga.

No matter which of the four main Hawaiian islands you are visiting, there are several traditional luaus to choose from. What all Hawaiian luaus have in common is a big all-you-care-to-eat luau buffet dinner. Sample popular luau delicacies, such as kalua roast pig, lomi lomi salmon and fresh island fish, or savor continental favorites like prime rib and chicken.

Prior to the big feast, you are invited to watch or participate in Hawaiian arts & crafts and other activities, such as hula and ukulele lessons and flower lei making. Following these island games is the traditional imu ceremony, in which the kalua pua'a (roast pig) is removed from the imu (underground oven). The grand finale of every Hawaiian luau celebration is a Polynesian show, which typically features music and dances from the Islands and includes the breathtaking Samoan fire-knife dance.
